## 1. System Classification

**System Type:** Hybrid — Content Generation + Conversational Assistant

**Description:**
Briefy's Phase 3 AI system accelerates agency work on creative demands by (a) generating structured content briefs from demand metadata, (b) providing a contextual chat assistant per demand that streams responses and remembers client patterns via `client_ai_memory`, and (c) generating full monthly content plans from client quota configuration. Users are marketing-agency team members (planners, account leads, content creators). "Good" means: the brief captures the demand's intent in the client's established tone within ~10 seconds; the chat answers questions grounded in the demand/client context without the user re-explaining; and the monthly plan fills the exact `monthly_posts` quota with realistic, channel-appropriate suggestions matching `monthly_plan_notes`.

**Critical Failure Modes:**
1. **Brand-voice drift** — Brief or chat output violates the client's established tone (stored in `client_ai_memory`), producing content the agency would not send to the client.
2. **Context hallucination** — Chat references demand files, comments, or client history that do not exist, fabricating facts about past interactions.
3. **Quota violation** — Monthly plan returns fewer or more items than `monthly_posts`, or distributes channels incorrectly when `monthly_plan_notes` specifies a breakdown.
4. **Streaming failure** — SSE stream terminates mid-response, leaves UI in loading state, or concatenates chunks out of order, resulting in broken/truncated output saved to DB.
5. **Memory poisoning** — Auto-extracted insights written to `client_ai_memory` with wrong client scope or low-confidence noise that degrades future generations (cross-client contamination).

## 1b. Domain Context

> Researched by `gsd-domain-researcher`. Grounds the evaluation strategy in domain expert knowledge.

**Industry Vertical:** Marketing & Creative Agencies (B2B SaaS). Briefy serves marketing/creative agencies and freelancers in Brazil (primarily pt-BR, with en/es surfaces) who deliver content production services to their own end-clients (brands). The AI never faces the end-client directly — it is an internal productivity layer for agency teams.

**User Population:** Agency professionals operating the platform on behalf of multiple brand clients:
- **Account Manager / Atendimento** — owns the client relationship, interprets brand strategy, reviews every brief before it reaches the client. Primary reviewer of AI output.
- **Content Strategist / Planner (Planejamento)** — designs monthly content plans, decides channel mix, content pillars, cadence. Primary consumer of `MonthlyPlanGenerator`.
- **Copywriter / Redator** — turns briefs into finished copy; judges whether an AI brief is actionable or needs rework.
- **Social Media / Community Manager** — executes the monthly plan, flags when AI-suggested items don't fit the platform.
- **Creative Director / Head de Criação** — final quality gate on tone, concept, and brand-voice fit before anything leaves the agency.

Agency clients (the brands) do **not** interact with the AI. They consume its downstream output (finished content produced from AI briefs and AI-generated plans) and will only ever see the AI indirectly through the agency's deliverables.

**Stakes Level:** **High** (two-tier stakes)
- **Tier 1 — Agency ↔ Brand-Client Relationship:** Content sent to the brand is evaluated against that brand's voice, strategy, and expectations. Off-brand or generic output threatens contract renewal and agency reputation — the primary revenue mechanism of the business.
- **Tier 2 — Individual Deliverable:** A single bad brief costs rework cycles (hours of copywriter/editor time). A bad monthly plan misaligns an entire month of production across multiple channels and team members. Neither tier is life-safety, but both have direct financial consequence and are visible to paying clients.

Stakes are mitigated by a mandatory human-in-the-loop gate: every AI brief is reviewed and edited by the Copywriter/Account Manager before reaching the brand, and every planning item requires explicit conversion (`convert to demand`) or acceptance. The AI is positioned as an assistant, not an auto-publisher — this is the single most important stakes-reducing design choice.

**Output Consequence:**
- **Brief output →** Copywriter reads brief → writes final copy based on it → Account Manager reviews → copy is sent to brand-client → (if aligned) becomes a production piece; (if misaligned) triggers rework cycle and silent trust erosion with the brand.
- **Chat output →** Used to unblock the team during ideation and to answer "how does this client usually do X?". Does not leave the agency, but poisons `client_ai_memory` if it asserts false patterns that then seed every subsequent brief for that client.
- **Monthly plan output →** Planner reviews plan → converts a subset into real demands → demands become the production backbone of an entire calendar month across all channels. A wrong channel mix or quota miss propagates to every team member's workload for 30 days.

### Known Failure Modes in This Domain

Domain-specific manifestations — how generic AI problems show up in the marketing-agency context. These go beyond the system-level critical failure modes in Section 1 (which focus on infrastructure: streaming, memory poisoning, quota math) and surface the **content-quality** failures the Account Manager or Creative Director will catch.

1. **"ChatGPT voice" homogenization.** AI defaults to a neutral, LinkedIn-corporate register regardless of client — uses "alavancar", "desbloquear", "potencializar", em-dashes, triads, and hedging adverbs. Looks competent to someone who hasn't read the client's previous content; immediately rejected by the Account Manager who has. This is the most common failure mode on first generation, before `client_ai_memory` has accumulated enough signal.

2. **Mid-piece voice drift.** First paragraph of a brief nails the client's tone; by paragraph four, the output has regressed to generic marketing prose. Happens because the system prompt's tone instructions have lower salience than the model's training distribution once generation momentum builds. Detectable only on a full read-through — a spot-check of the opening misses it.

3. **Plan monoculture (pillar collapse).** Monthly plan returns the requested `monthly_posts` count, but all 20 items are the same format ("X dicas para Y") with a number and a noun swapped. Passes the quota check but fails the "would a planner actually send this to a client?" check. Frequent when the prompt over-emphasises quantity without channel/pillar diversity rules.

4. **Channel-format mismatch.** Brief for a `channel='instagram'` demand proposes a 1200-word structure with H2 headers, or an Instagram carousel with 15 slides (platform supports 10). Brief for `channel='linkedin'` uses Instagram hashtag-heavy conventions. The channel field is in the prompt, but its implications (length, format, platform conventions) are not enforced.

5. **Cross-client memory contamination.** `client_ai_memory` insight extracted during Chat with Client A is persisted with Client B's `client_id` (usually a bug: wrong scoping in `ExtractClientMemoryJob`, or user switched clients mid-chat and memory writer didn't re-read the current scope). Next brief for Client B opens with Client A's signature phrasing. Silent failure — no visible error, just "esse cliente não fala assim" from the Account Manager weeks later.

6. **Low-confidence insight pollution.** Memory extractor writes marginal observations ("cliente usou a palavra 'incrível' uma vez") as if they were patterns. Over time, `client_ai_memory` fills with noise that lowers the signal-to-noise ratio of the chat system prompt, causing *all* subsequent generations for that client to degrade. A `confidence < 0.6` drop-threshold in the extractor mitigates this; the gsd-eval-planner should make this a monitored metric.

7. **Seasonal/commemorative-date blindness.** Monthly plan for May ignores Dia das Mães (second-biggest retail date in Brazil after Black Friday). Plan for June ignores Festa Junina. For some verticals (beauty, food, retail) these are the highest-performing content opportunities of the month. The AI has no awareness of the Brazilian commemorative-date calendar unless prompted.

### Regulatory / Compliance Context

Briefy is not vertical-locked — an agency on Briefy may serve healthcare, financial-services, alcohol, or other regulated end-clients. The platform itself does not enforce sector compliance, but its AI output must not create compliance exposure for the agency.

**LGPD (Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados — Brazil, Lei 13.709/2018, strengthened 2026 by Lei 15.352/2026 "LGPD 2.0").**
- In agency/brand relationships, the **brand is controller, the agency is operator**, and Briefy — by hosting `client_ai_memory`, chat history, and demand data — is a **sub-operator**.
- `client_ai_memory` must **never** store personal data of end-consumers (brand's customers). It stores tone/pattern/preference insights about the brand-client, not about individuals. Extraction prompts for `ClientMemoryExtractor` should refuse to persist PII.
- Chat transcripts and `ai_conversation_messages` must be scoped to the organization and client — cross-tenant leakage is an LGPD breach notification event under ANPD's 2026 rules.
- Data sent to Anthropic's API is an international data transfer. Per Briefy's architecture (`STATE.md`), Anthropic is a sub-processor; this must appear in the Briefy ↔ agency data-processing agreement.

**CONAR (Conselho Nacional de Autorregulamentação Publicitária).**
- CONAR code requires advertising to be clear, truthful, and identifiable as advertising regardless of production technology. AI-assisted content is not exempt.
- Relevant to Briefy: the brief generator must not invent factual claims about the client's product (e.g., "cientificamente comprovado", "o melhor do mercado") unless those claims are already in the demand's source material. A fabricated claim copied by the Copywriter into final copy becomes a CONAR complaint against the brand — and a liability concern for the agency.
- Under Brazilian doctrine (Scartezzini 2026), AI-generated advertising content is legally treated as if created by the advertiser's team — the agency is responsible.

**Sector-specific overlays (agency-dependent, not platform-enforced).**
- **Healthcare clients** → CFM (medical council) Resolution 2.126/2015 restricts therapeutic claims; ANVISA restricts medication advertising. Brief generator must not produce therapeutic promises.
- **Financial-services clients** → CVM and BCB rules on investment-product advertising; required disclaimers.
- **Alcohol clients** → CONAR Anexo "P" restricts alcohol advertising audience, claims, and contexts.
- **Children-facing content** → CONAR Anexo "H" + CDC restrictions on advertising to children under 12.

Briefy does not attempt to enforce sector rules inside the AI — that is the Account Manager's expertise. But the system prompt and eval rubric should flag obvious violations (therapeutic claims, guaranteed-returns language, etc.) as a secondary guardrail. This is explicit scope for Section 6 (Guardrails) and a constraint the `gsd-eval-planner` must surface.

## 2. Framework Decision

**Selected Framework:** Anthropic PHP SDK (`anthropics/anthropic-sdk-php`) — direct API calls

**Version:** To be pinned at implementation time — use latest stable ^0.x in `composer require anthropics/anthropic-sdk-php` (validate compatibility with PHP 8.4 before committing lockfile).

**Rationale:**
The backend stack is PHP 8.4 + Laravel 13 + Inertia.js — every Python/TypeScript AI framework in the standard decision matrix (LangChain, LlamaIndex, CrewAI, LangGraph, OpenAI Agents SDK, Claude Agent SDK) is architecturally incompatible. Within the PHP ecosystem the real choice is **direct SDK** vs. **Prism PHP abstraction**. The three AI capabilities (brief generation, streaming chat, monthly planning) are single-turn generation or simple multi-turn streaming calls — not stateful agent graphs, not RAG with retrieval pipelines, not multi-agent handoffs. Direct SDK use gives (1) full control over SSE stream construction to feed Laravel's streaming response into React's EventSource; (2) zero abstraction overhead between the controller and Claude's API; (3) explicit token/cost/latency visibility for each of the five eval concerns; (4) simpler debugging paths because every HTTP call is inspectable. The team has already captured this decision in STATE.md.

**Alternatives Considered:**

| Framework | Ruled Out Because |
|-----------|------------------|
| Prism PHP (`echolabsdev/prism ^0.100.1`, already in `composer.json`) | Provides fluent Laravel-native multi-provider abstraction; unnecessary for a single-provider (Anthropic-only) implementation with custom SSE streaming. Kept as a resolved dormant dependency — reactivating is a 1-commit future change if multi-provider becomes a requirement. |
| LangChain / LlamaIndex / CrewAI / LangGraph | Python-only, incompatible with PHP backend. Would require a separate service (microservice or sidecar) — adds deployment, transport, and operational complexity not warranted by the use cases. |
| OpenAI Agents SDK / Claude Agent SDK | Python + TypeScript only; also agent-oriented, which overshoots the single-turn content generation and multi-turn chat patterns here. |
| Direct HTTP (Guzzle/Http facade, no SDK) | Loses SDK-provided SSE event parsing, retry logic, and schema validation for request/response shapes. Net time saved by SDK > cost of the dependency. |

**Vendor Lock-In Accepted:** Yes — full Anthropic/Claude lock-in. Deliberate trade-off per CONTEXT.md: single-provider keeps streaming reliability and prompt tuning simple. If multi-provider support becomes required in a later milestone (v1.2+), Prism PHP is already in `composer.json` and can replace direct SDK calls with minimal refactoring.

### Entry Point Pattern

Hybrid system — three entry points. All three use the same `Client::messages` API, with only `createStream()` vs. `create()` differing.

**(a) Brief generation — streaming controller (single-turn, SSE to React).**

```php
<?php
// app/Http/Controllers/AiBriefController.php
namespace App\Http\Controllers;

use Anthropic\Client;
use App\Models\Demand;
use App\Services\Ai\BriefPromptBuilder;
use Illuminate\Http\StreamedEvent;
use Symfony\Component\HttpFoundation\StreamedResponse;

class AiBriefController
{
    public function __construct(
        private Client $anthropic,
        private BriefPromptBuilder $prompts,
    ) {}

    public function generate(Demand $demand): StreamedResponse
    {
        $this->authorize('update', $demand);

        // Compose system + user prompts from demand metadata + client memory.
        [$system, $userMessage] = $this->prompts->build($demand);

        return response()->eventStream(function () use ($system, $userMessage, $demand) {
            $buffer = '';

            $stream = $this->anthropic->messages->createStream(
                maxTokens: 2048,
                messages: [['role' => 'user', 'content' => $userMessage]],
                model: config('services.anthropic.model_chat'),
                system: $system, // array with cache_control on the stable prefix
                temperature: 0.7,
            );

            // The SDK iterator yields one parsed SSE event per iteration.
            foreach ($stream as $event) {
                if ($event->type === 'content_block_delta'
                    && $event->delta->type === 'text_delta') {
                    $chunk = $event->delta->text;
                    $buffer .= $chunk;
                    yield new StreamedEvent(event: 'delta', data: json_encode(['text' => $chunk]));
                }

                if ($event->type === 'message_stop') {
                    // Persist final brief in demands.ai_analysis.brief once stream completes.
                    $demand->update([
                        'ai_analysis' => array_merge(
                            (array) $demand->ai_analysis,
                            ['brief' => $buffer, 'brief_generated_at' => now()->toIso8601String()],
                        ),
                    ]);
                    yield new StreamedEvent(event: 'done', data: json_encode(['ok' => true]));
                }
            }
        });
        // response()->eventStream() automatically sets text/event-stream, disables
        // nginx buffering (X-Accel-Buffering: no), and emits </stream> on generator return.
    }
}
```

**(b) Chat — streaming, multi-turn, history from `ai_conversation_messages`.**

```php
public function stream(Demand $demand, AiConversation $conversation, ChatRequest $req): StreamedResponse
{
    $this->authorize('view', $demand);

    $system  = $this->prompts->chatSystem($demand);          // cached prefix
    $history = $conversation->messages()->orderBy('id')->get()
        ->map(fn ($m) => ['role' => $m->role, 'content' => $m->content])->all();

    // Persist user turn BEFORE opening the stream (so it survives client disconnects).
    $conversation->messages()->create(['role' => 'user', 'content' => $req->input('message')]);
    $history[] = ['role' => 'user', 'content' => $req->input('message')];

    return response()->eventStream(function () use ($system, $history, $conversation) {
        $assistant = '';
        $stream = $this->anthropic->messages->createStream(
            maxTokens: 2048,
            messages: $history,
            model: config('services.anthropic.model_chat'),
            system: $system,
            temperature: 0.7,
        );

        foreach ($stream as $event) {
            if ($event->type === 'content_block_delta' && $event->delta->type === 'text_delta') {
                $assistant .= $event->delta->text;
                yield new StreamedEvent(event: 'delta', data: json_encode(['text' => $event->delta->text]));
            }
            if ($event->type === 'message_stop') {
                $conversation->messages()->create(['role' => 'assistant', 'content' => $assistant]);
                // Dispatch background job to extract client_ai_memory insights (D-14).
                \App\Jobs\ExtractClientMemoryJob::dispatch($conversation->fresh());
                yield new StreamedEvent(event: 'done', data: json_encode(['ok' => true]));
            }
        }
    });
}
```

### Key Abstractions

| Concept | What It Is | When You Use It |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| `Anthropic\Client` | Top-level HTTP client. Named-parameter API (`messages->create(...)`). | Resolved once via container singleton; injected into every AI service. |
| `messages->create()` vs. `messages->createStream()` | Blocking vs. streaming Messages API. Same parameters; stream yields SSE events. | `create()` for planning + redesign (structured output). `createStream()` for brief + chat (typewriter UX per D-04, D-11). |
| Stream event types | `message_start`, `content_block_start`, `content_block_delta` (with `text_delta` / `input_json_delta`), `content_block_stop`, `message_delta`, `message_stop`, `ping`, `error` | Filter on `content_block_delta` + `text_delta` for user-visible text; watch `error` for provider-side failures mid-stream. |
| `cache_control: {type: 'ephemeral'}` | Server-side prompt cache (5-min default, 1-hour optional at 2× write cost). Read cost is 10% of input. | Placed on the stable prefix of the chat `system` prompt (client_ai_memory + demand metadata + file list) — amortised across 10+ turns of one conversation. |
| `response()->eventStream(Closure): StreamedResponse` | Laravel 13 helper that wraps a generator in a `text/event-stream` response, auto-flushes, sets `X-Accel-Buffering: no`, and emits `</stream>` on completion. Consumed by React via `EventSource`. | Every streaming endpoint (brief generate, chat stream). Yield `StreamedEvent(event: 'delta'|'done'|'error', data: $json)`. |
| `APIException` hierarchy | `RateLimitException` (429), `APITimeoutException`, `APIConnectionException`, `APIStatusException` (other non-2xx). Retries already built in (2× default). | Wrap every SDK call; on `RateLimitException` return HTTP 429 to client with `Retry-After` so React can back off. |

### Common Pitfalls

1. **Wrong package name.** Running `composer require anthropics/anthropic-sdk-php` fails — that's the GitHub slug. The Packagist package is **`anthropic-ai/sdk`**. Silent footgun: new developers paste the GitHub URL into composer.json and get a 404.
2. **PHP-FPM buffers SSE by default.** `response()->eventStream()` sets `X-Accel-Buffering: no`, but if you put the endpoint behind FastCGI or a proxy with response buffering (Apache mod_deflate, Cloudflare "Rocket Loader", nginx `proxy_buffering on`) the client sees the full response at once and the typewriter effect breaks. Required: `fastcgi_buffering off;` and `gzip off;` on the SSE locations.
3. **`max_execution_time` kills long streams.** A 30–60 s brief/chat stream exceeds PHP's default `max_execution_time=30`. Set `set_time_limit(0)` at the top of the streaming controller (or configure it in `php.ini` for the SSE routes). Also set a generous client `timeout` on the `Client` constructor (we use 120 s) — otherwise the SDK cancels mid-stream.
4. **Streaming is mandatory above ~21k output tokens.** Anthropic's non-streaming HTTP endpoint hard-times-out on long completions. Use `createStream()` and accumulate — even if you ultimately render the full message, streaming avoids 529/timeout failures on the monthly plan endpoint when `monthly_posts` is large.
5. **Cache breakpoint drift invalidates the cache.** `cache_control: {type: 'ephemeral'}` only hits when the prefix is **byte-identical**. Any timestamp, `now()->toIso8601String()`, or variable ordering in the cached block invalidates the entry and silently doubles your cost (you pay the 1.25× write price without ever reading it back). Verify with `usage.cache_read_input_tokens > 0` on the second call.
6. **Partial JSON in `tool_use` / structured output.** `input_json_delta` events carry **partial JSON strings** (not objects) — never `json_decode` a delta. Accumulate all deltas for a given `index`, then decode on `content_block_stop`. This also applies if we use tool-use to force structured insights extraction for `client_ai_memory` (D-14).
7. **Cumulative usage fields.** `message_delta.usage.output_tokens` is **cumulative**, not incremental. Reading it naively and summing gives wildly wrong token counts. Read `usage` once from `message_stop` or from the final `message_delta`.

## 4. Implementation Guidance

**Model Configuration:**

Three tiers, explicitly matched to the cognitive complexity of each capability. The rule: **Opus 4.7 on tasks that require reasoning over many constraints at once; Sonnet 4.6 on structured generation with a clear template; Haiku 4.5 on narrow extraction/summarisation.**

| Use Case | Model | `max_tokens` | `temperature` | Why this tier |
|----------|-------|--------------|--------------|---------------|
| **Monthly plan (non-streaming)** | **`claude-opus-4-7`** | 4096 | 0.4 | **Complex reasoning task** — the model must (1) distribute exactly `monthly_posts` items, (2) respect `monthly_plan_notes` channel breakdown, (3) enforce thematic diversity (no pillar monoculture), (4) stay on brand voice per `client_ai_memory`, (5) spread items realistically across the month. This is the AI capability where quality failures are most expensive (a bad plan drives a month of wrong production). Worth the ~5× input / ~5× output cost over Sonnet — 10 plans/month is a trivial volume line item. |
| Brief generation (streaming) | `claude-sonnet-4-6` | 2048 | 0.7 | Single-turn structured content with a clear template (objective, tone, channel, CTA). Sonnet handles brand-voice-sensitive output well; Opus is overkill here and would double TTFT. |
| Chat (streaming, multi-turn) | `claude-sonnet-4-6` | 2048 | 0.7 | Grounded reasoning over demand + files + memory. Sonnet handles the 10–30k-token cached prefix comfortably. **Future (v1.2):** optional router that escalates to Opus when the user asks explicitly for strategic/planning help inside the chat. Out of scope for Phase 3. |
| Item redesign (non-streaming, low-stakes) | `claude-haiku-4-5` | 512 | 0.7 | Single-item rewrite with short feedback. Haiku's $1/$5 per MTok vs. Sonnet's $3/$15 is a 3× saving on the high-volume UX-latency-critical path. |
| Memory extraction (non-streaming, background job) | `claude-haiku-4-5` | 1024 | 0.2 | Narrow structured extraction with tool-use schema validation. Runs off the hot path in a queue worker. |
| Conversation compaction (background job) | `claude-haiku-4-5` | 800 | 0.3 | Summarisation is Haiku's sweet spot; triggered when `messages.count() > 30`. |

**Core Pattern:**

Streaming + persistence + memory update — the lifecycle that matters for brief and chat:

```php
// app/Services/Ai/ChatPromptBuilder.php — system prompt composition per D-12.
final class ChatPromptBuilder
{
    public function chatSystem(Demand $demand): array
    {
        // Load related data. Eager-load on the controller side to avoid N+1 here.
        $client   = $demand->client;
        $files    = $demand->files->map(fn ($f) => "- {$f->name} ({$f->mime_type})")->implode("\n");
        $comments = $demand->comments()->latest()->take(20)->get()
                        ->reverse()
                        ->map(fn ($c) => "[{$c->created_at}] {$c->user->name}: {$c->body}")
                        ->implode("\n");
        $memory   = $client->aiMemory()->orderBy('category')->get()
                        ->map(fn ($m) => "- [{$m->category}] ({$m->confidence}) {$m->insight}")
                        ->implode("\n");

        // Two system blocks. The first is stable per (client, demand) and cacheable.
        // The second holds the fast-changing comments tail (last 20) — outside the cache.
        return [
            [
                'type' => 'text',
                'text' => <<<PROMPT
                You are Briefy's AI assistant for a marketing agency. You help the team work on a specific creative demand.

                ## Client: {$client->name}
                Tone of voice, patterns and preferences (from past work):
                {$memory}

                ## Demand
                Title: {$demand->title}
                Objective: {$demand->objective}
                Channel: {$demand->channel}
                Deadline: {$demand->deadline}
                Description:
                {$demand->description}

                ## Files attached to the demand
                {$files}

                ## Rules
                - Only reference files, comments, memory entries or fields present above. Never invent past interactions.
                - Stay within the client's tone. If unclear, ask before writing final copy.
                - Output in Portuguese-BR unless the user writes in English.
                PROMPT,
                'cache_control' => ['type' => 'ephemeral'], // 5-min cache — covers a typical chat session
            ],
            [
                'type' => 'text',
                'text' => "## Last 20 comments on this demand\n{$comments}",
                // NO cache_control here — this list changes every time a comment is added.
            ],
        ];
    }
}
```

The same `cache_control` pattern applies to brief and planning system prompts. The key rule: **cache what's stable per entity, keep volatile tails outside the cached block**. Cache hits cost 10% of input price; a 15k-token system prompt costs $0.045 on write, $0.0045 on every subsequent read.

**Tool Use:**

Claude native tools are **not** required for Phase 3 — we do not wire Claude to filesystem, DB, or external APIs; instead we pre-compute all context and put it in the system prompt. Tool use is, however, the recommended transport for **structured insight extraction** (D-14):

```php

$response = $this->anthropic->messages->create(
    maxTokens: 1024,
    messages: [['role' => 'user', 'content' => $transcript]],
    model: config('services.anthropic.model_cheap'),
    system: 'You extract durable client-voice insights from a chat transcript. Call record_insights exactly once.',
    tools: [[
        'name' => 'record_insights',
        'description' => 'Record one or more durable insights about the client.',
        'input_schema' => [
            'type' => 'object',
            'properties' => [
                'insights' => [
                    'type' => 'array',
                    'items' => [
                        'type' => 'object',
                        'properties' => [
                            'category'   => ['type' => 'string', 'enum' => ['tone', 'patterns', 'preferences', 'avoid', 'terminology']],
                            'insight'    => ['type' => 'string', 'maxLength' => 280],
                            'confidence' => ['type' => 'number', 'minimum' => 0, 'maximum' => 1],
                        ],
                        'required' => ['category', 'insight', 'confidence'],
                    ],
                ],
            ],
            'required' => ['insights'],
        ],
    ]],
    toolChoice: ['type' => 'tool', 'name' => 'record_insights'], // forces the tool call
    temperature: 0.2,
);

// Extract the tool_use block (schema-validated JSON, not a text envelope).
$toolUse = collect($response->content)->firstWhere('type', 'tool_use');
$insights = $toolUse->input['insights'] ?? [];
// Drop low-confidence entries; upsert the rest into client_ai_memory (scoped to client_id).
```

This is strictly more reliable than asking Claude to return JSON in a text block and then regex-parsing — the tool_use schema is validated server-side and the `input` field is a parsed object (not a string).

**State Management:**

| State | Where it lives | Refresh trigger |
|-------|---------------|----------------|
| Brief text | `demands.ai_analysis->brief` (JSON column, existing) | Written on `message_stop` inside the controller's generator. |
| Chat history | `ai_conversations` + `ai_conversation_messages` tables | User turn persisted **before** opening the stream. Assistant turn persisted on `message_stop`. Both inside the controller. |
| Conversation compaction marker | New column `ai_conversations.compacted_at` (add to migration) | When `messages.count() > 30`, `CompactConversationJob` summarises the first N messages into one system message, deletes them, records timestamp. |
| Client insights | `client_ai_memory` (existing) | `ExtractClientMemoryJob` dispatched on `message_stop`. Upsert by `(client_id, category, insight_hash)` to avoid duplicates. |
| Planning suggestions | `planning_suggestions` (existing) | Written synchronously at end of `MonthlyPlanGenerator::generate()` inside a DB transaction. |
| Stream cursor / partial text | **In-memory only** in the generator scope | No durable "in-progress" state; on client disconnect we lose the partial response. Acceptable for typewriter UX. |

**Context Window Strategy:**

Claude Sonnet 4.6 gives 200k tokens; we are nowhere near that. The realistic concern is **cost**, not context limit.

- **Brief generation:** ~2k–4k input (demand + minimal client memory) + ~1.5k output. Single call. No history.
- **Chat:** ~10k–20k stable system prefix (memory + demand + files) + growing `messages[]` array. Cached prefix keeps per-turn cost flat.
- **Chat growth:** D-13 — at >30 messages, `ConversationCompactor` runs: takes messages 1..(N-10), asks Haiku to produce a 400-token summary, replaces them with a single `{role: user, content: "Resumo da conversa: ..."}` seed, and keeps the last 10 turns verbatim. This cuts input tokens back to ~15k + 10 turns and preserves the UX suggestion "start a new conversation" surfaced to the user.
- **Monthly plan:** One call, ~3k–6k input, up to 4k output. No history, no truncation logic needed.

## 4b. AI Systems Best Practices

> Written by `gsd-ai-researcher`. Cross-cutting patterns every developer building AI systems needs — independent of framework choice.

### Structured Outputs with JSON Schema + Laravel Validation

> PHP equivalent of the Python "Pydantic contract" pattern. Define the output shape as a Laravel `FormRequest` / validator rule set; force Claude to emit that shape via **tool-use** (preferred) or JSON-mode prompting; validate on receipt; retry on failure.

**Why it matters here.** Monthly plan (D-20…D-22) must return exactly `clients.monthly_posts` items with stable field names (`date`, `title`, `description`, inferred `channel`). Insight extraction (D-14) must produce a category enum + a confidence score in [0, 1]. Unvalidated LLM JSON is quota-violation failure mode #3 waiting to happen.

**Preferred: tool-use as the structured-output transport.**

```php
// app/Services/Ai/Schemas/MonthlyPlanSchema.php
final class MonthlyPlanSchema
{
    /** JSON schema passed to Claude's tool input_schema. */
    public static function toolSchema(int $expectedCount): array
    {
        return [
            'type' => 'object',
            'properties' => [
                'items' => [
                    'type' => 'array',
                    'minItems' => $expectedCount,
                    'maxItems' => $expectedCount,
                    'items' => [
                        'type' => 'object',
                        'properties' => [
                            'date'        => ['type' => 'string', 'format' => 'date'],
                            'title'       => ['type' => 'string', 'minLength' => 3, 'maxLength' => 140],
                            'description' => ['type' => 'string', 'minLength' => 10, 'maxLength' => 600],
                            'channel'     => ['type' => 'string', 'enum' => ['instagram', 'linkedin', 'facebook', 'tiktok', 'blog', 'email', 'other']],
                        ],
                        'required' => ['date', 'title', 'description', 'channel'],
                    ],
                ],
            ],
            'required' => ['items'],
        ];
    }

    /** Defensive second gate: Laravel validator rules applied AFTER the SDK returns. */
    public static function rules(int $expectedCount): array
    {
        return [
            'items'               => ['required', 'array', "size:{$expectedCount}"],
            'items.*.date'        => ['required', 'date'],
            'items.*.title'       => ['required', 'string', 'min:3', 'max:140'],
            'items.*.description' => ['required', 'string', 'min:10', 'max:600'],
            'items.*.channel'     => ['required', Rule::in(['instagram', 'linkedin', 'facebook', 'tiktok', 'blog', 'email', 'other'])],
        ];
    }
}
```

**Retry logic — at most 2 retries, with feedback loop.**

```php
// app/Services/Ai/MonthlyPlanGenerator.php
public function generate(Client $client, int $year, int $month): array
{
    $attempts = 0;
    $lastError = null;

    while ($attempts < 3) {
        $attempts++;
        try {
            $response = $this->anthropic->messages->create(
                maxTokens: 4096,
                messages: $this->buildMessages($client, $year, $month, $lastError),
                // Opus 4.7 — monthly planning is the complex-reasoning capability.
                // See Section 4 "Model Configuration" for the routing policy.
                model: config('services.anthropic.model_complex'),
                system: $this->prompts->planSystem($client),
                tools: [[
                    'name' => 'submit_plan',
                    'description' => "Submit exactly {$client->monthly_posts} items for the month.",
                    'input_schema' => MonthlyPlanSchema::toolSchema($client->monthly_posts),
                ]],
                toolChoice: ['type' => 'tool', 'name' => 'submit_plan'],
                temperature: 0.4,
            );

            $toolUse = collect($response->content)->firstWhere('type', 'tool_use');
            abort_if(!$toolUse, 502, 'Model did not call submit_plan');

            // Second-gate Laravel validation — catches schema drift the model's
            // client-side validator missed (rare but real).
            return validator(
                $toolUse->input,
                MonthlyPlanSchema::rules($client->monthly_posts),
            )->validate();

        } catch (ValidationException $e) {
            // Feed the specific error back to the model on next attempt.
            $lastError = $e->errors();
            Log::warning('monthly_plan.validation_failed', [
                'client_id' => $client->id, 'attempt' => $attempts, 'errors' => $lastError,
            ]);
        } catch (RateLimitException $e) {
            throw $e; // bubble up — SDK already retried twice
        }
    }

    Log::error('monthly_plan.max_retries_exceeded', [
        'client_id' => $client->id, 'last_error' => $lastError,
    ]);
    throw new RuntimeException('Monthly plan generation failed schema validation 3×.');
}
```

**What to log on each attempt:** `client_id`, `attempt_number`, `last_error`, `response.usage` (tokens + cost), `duration_ms`. What to surface: only the final failure, with a user-facing message "Não foi possível gerar o planejamento. Tente novamente." — never expose the raw JSON-schema error to agency users.

For brief and chat (free-form prose), we do **not** force a JSON shape — the only structural constraint is "render as markdown" and we validate by length + non-empty.

### Async-First Design (PHP / Laravel edition)

PHP is not async-first like Node or Python. The equivalents we rely on are:

1. **Streaming responses** (`response()->eventStream()`) — the HTTP process stays alive and yields chunks as they arrive from Anthropic. This is the ONLY "async" surface the user sees on brief and chat.
2. **Queue jobs** for anything the user doesn't need to wait for.

**The one common mistake in this codebase:** running a 60-second `messages->create()` synchronously inside a controller that wasn't declared streaming. PHP-FPM workers are pool-sized (typically 10–30). One slow AI call per worker ties up a slot. For anything > 3 s or anything the user doesn't need to watch, **dispatch a job**:

```php
// Correct: memory extraction runs AFTER the chat stream has closed — user doesn't wait.
\App\Jobs\ExtractClientMemoryJob::dispatch($conversation)->onQueue('ai');

// Correct: long monthly plan generation for large quotas — show a "generating" state
// in the UI and poll a job-status endpoint. Or use broadcasting (Reverb is already installed).
GenerateMonthlyPlanJob::dispatch($client, $year, $month)->onQueue('ai');
```

`.env`:
```
QUEUE_CONNECTION=redis  # NOT sync for AI jobs; database queue driver is also fine but slower
```

Run a dedicated worker for the `ai` queue (higher timeout, lower concurrency):
```bash
php artisan queue:work --queue=ai --timeout=300 --tries=2 --backoff=30
```

**Stream vs. await decision rule:**
- **Stream** (`createStream()` + `eventStream()`) when the user is staring at a spinner — brief generation, chat. UX demands immediate first-token feedback.
- **Await** (`create()` + job) when the output must be validated/transformed before display — monthly plan (we can't show half a schema), memory extraction (no UI), redesign (512-token output is fast enough to feel instant without streaming).

### Prompt Engineering Discipline

1. **System vs. user separation — always use the `system` parameter.** Never concatenate system instructions into the `user` message. Claude is trained to treat `system` as higher-priority and more trustworthy than `user`. System prompt holds: role, client memory, demand metadata, file list, tone rules. User prompt holds: just the current turn's request.

2. **Few-shot: inline in the system prompt, not retrieved.** We have no vector store and the use case is narrow enough that 1–2 canonical examples per capability (brief / plan / redesign) are sufficient. Store them as versioned text files under `resources/prompts/` and load into `BriefPromptBuilder`:

   ```
   resources/prompts/
   ├── brief_system.md
   ├── brief_fewshot_instagram.md
   ├── plan_system.md
   └── redesign_system.md
   ```

   Versioning: hash the file contents and include the hash in the trace log — so when a prompt changes, we can attribute eval-score drift to the change.

3. **`max_tokens` is always explicit, never the SDK default.**
   - Brief: 2048 (~1500 words)
   - Chat: 2048 per turn
   - Plan: 4096 (scales with `monthly_posts`; verify against `monthly_posts × ~150 tokens/item` + buffer)
   - Redesign: 512 (single item — anything longer is a prompt problem)
   - Memory extraction: 1024 (tool-use output — tight schema)

   A runaway `max_tokens` is both a cost bug (a 16k-token brief costs 8× a 2k brief) **and** a latency bug (first-token latency is not affected, but total stream duration scales linearly).

4. **Never include secrets or API keys in prompts.** Obvious, but worth stating: `client_ai_memory` is scoped per `client_id`; cross-client memory leakage is failure mode #5.

### Context Window Management

System type is **Hybrid — conversational + content generation**. The relevant patterns:

**Conversational (chat, D-09..D-14):**
- **Strategy:** summarisation-based compaction. No sliding window — agency users refer back to the start of a conversation when iterating on a brief.
- **Trigger:** `count(messages) > 30` OR `estimated_tokens > 50_000` (whichever first). Use `CompactConversationJob` — NOT in the request path.
- **Algorithm:** Take messages 1..(N-10), call Haiku with a summarisation prompt, produce a 400-token "Resumo da conversa anterior: ..." seed. Replace the original messages atomically in a DB transaction. Keep the last 10 turns verbatim. Set `ai_conversations.compacted_at = now()` and surface the "start a new conversation?" suggestion in the UI (D-13).
- **Cache implication:** Compaction invalidates the cache entry (the system prefix is unchanged, but the `messages[]` prefix isn't cached anyway). No action needed.

**Content Generation (brief, plan, redesign):**
- **Strategy:** single-turn. No history to manage. The whole risk is input size of the cached system prefix.
- **Guard:** if `len(client_ai_memory) + len(files) + len(comments) > 30_000 tokens`, truncate comments to the most recent 10 and keep the full memory block. Log the truncation.

**Prompt cache (cross-cutting):**
- Every multi-turn capability (chat only, in Phase 3) uses `cache_control: {type: 'ephemeral'}` on the system prefix.
- 5-minute TTL matches typical agency working patterns — a user usually iterates 5–15 turns in one sitting. Use 1-hour TTL (`{type: 'ephemeral', ttl: '1h'}`, 2× write cost) only if metrics show users pausing >5 min between turns.
- On the **first** call of a session, expect `usage.cache_creation_input_tokens > 0` (125% cost). On turns 2..N, expect `usage.cache_read_input_tokens > 0` (10% cost). Assert this in tests.
- **Minimum cacheable block size (enforced silently by the API):** Opus 4.7 = **4,096 tokens**; Haiku 4.5 = **4,096 tokens**; Sonnet 4.6 = 1,024–2,048 tokens. Sub-threshold prompts return 0 for both `cache_creation_input_tokens` and `cache_read_input_tokens` — no error is raised. Monitor these counters in tests and in Phoenix traces; a silently-uncached prompt is a cost regression waiting to happen.
- **Manual vs. automatic caching.** We use **manual** per-block `cache_control` on the stable system prefix (line-aligned with volatile tails outside the cache). The SDK also supports automatic caching (`cache_control: {type: 'ephemeral'}` at top level), where the breakpoint auto-advances across conversation turns. Manual gives us finer control (we know exactly which block is cached); automatic is cheaper to integrate but harder to reason about when we add a second cache block later. **Start manual; revisit automatic in Phase 4 if chat history management becomes complex.**
- **Breakpoint budget:** Anthropic allows at most **4 `cache_control` breakpoints per request**. Phase 3 uses 1 (stable system prefix). Combining manual + automatic would consume 2. Keep the budget in mind before adding more.

### Cost and Latency Budget

**Assumptions for estimates** (source: https://platform.claude.com/docs/en/build-with-claude/prompt-caching + Claude pricing page — all multipliers are relative to base input/output; validate exact $/MTok at implementation time as Anthropic pricing may shift):

| Claude Opus 4.7 | ~$15/MTok | ~$75/MTok | ~$1.50/MTok | ~$18.75/MTok | **4,096** |

Sonnet 4.6 is more forgiving (1–2k min).

**Caching tactics per capability:**

| Capability | Caching mode | Rationale |
|------------|-------------|-----------|
| Chat (multi-turn) | **Manual `cache_control` on the stable system prefix** (client memory + demand + files). Comments tail outside the cache. | Brief regeneration | `cache_control` on demand-metadata prefix. If user clicks Regenerate within 5 min (common), the cache hit is close to free. | Same TTL; regeneration latency drops from ~8s to ~5s. |
| Monthly plan (Opus) | **Verify prefix ≥ 4,096 tokens** before applying `cache_control`. If short, inline last 2 plans or last 10 briefs for the client to cross the threshold deliberately. | Plans within the same month often re-use the same client context; 1-hour TTL (`ttl: "1h"`, 2× write) is worth evaluating if user generates multiple months in one sitting. |
| Redesign (Haiku) | Skip explicit `cache_control` unless the per-item prefix crosses 4,096 tokens. At ~1k input, caching is silently inactive. | Don't waste a breakpoint on an uncacheable block. |
| Memory extraction / compaction | Skip caching — one-shot inputs. | Not reused. |

> **Max 4 `cache_control` breakpoints per request.** We use at most 1 (the stable system prefix). Automatic caching, if enabled, consumes another slot — plan for 2 used, 2 free for future extensions.

**Latency budget (p95 targets):**
- Time-to-first-token (brief, chat): **< 1.5 s**
- Full brief stream completion: **< 10 s** (goal in Section 1)
- Monthly plan (non-streaming): **< 15 s** for up to 30 items
- Redesign single item: **< 3 s**

If p95 exceeds these, inspect in order: (1) cache hit rate — missing cache doubles input processing time; (2) `max_tokens` — if set too high Claude over-generates; (3) network — we are PHP-to-api.anthropic.com synchronous.

**Sub-task model routing — explicit policy (three tiers):**

| Tier | Model | Capabilities | Reason |
|------|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Complex reasoning** | **Opus 4.7** | Monthly plan generation | Must juggle many constraints simultaneously (quota, channel mix, thematic diversity, brand voice, seasonal fit). Quality failures here drive a month of wrong production. Low volume (~10 plans/month) makes the cost premium trivial. |
| **Production default** | Sonnet 4.6 | Brief generation, Chat (multi-turn) | Structured single-turn generation with clear template (brief) and grounded multi-turn reasoning over a large cached prefix (chat). Opus would be overkill and would hurt TTFT. |
| **Auxiliary** | Haiku 4.5 | Redesign single item, Memory extraction, Conversation compaction | Narrow, well-defined tasks with schema gates. 3× cheaper than Sonnet; UX-critical latency on redesign; background for the other two. |

**Escalation hook (future, not Phase 3):** for a specific chat turn, if the controller detects an explicit strategy/planning ask (heuristic: keywords "planejar", "estratégia", "como devo estruturar", or the user is asking for a monthly plan inside chat), route that single turn to Opus 4.7 rather than the session default. Out of scope now; capture in backlog.

**Exact-match caching (HTTP-level, application layer):** optional Phase 4 enhancement. For brief generation specifically, hash `(demand.updated_at, client.ai_memory_updated_at)` as a cache key in Redis; if the user clicks "Regenerate" twice without changes, serve the stored `ai_analysis.brief` directly. Not in Phase 3 scope — noted here for the backlog.

## 5. Evaluation Strategy

> Grounded in Section 1b rubric ingredients (Dimensions 1–8) and Section 1 critical failure modes. Every dimension below traces back to a practitioner rubric and/or a named failure mode. Measurement approach is constrained by the PHP/Laravel stack — LLM-judge evals run via an artisan command against a seeded reference dataset; tracing is Arize Phoenix via OpenTelemetry (language-agnostic).

### Dimensions

Eleven dimensions. The first eight map 1:1 to Section 1b. The last three cover infrastructure-level critical failure modes (streaming, memory scope, quota budget) that are not content-quality rubrics but must be monitored.

| # | Dimension | Rubric | Measurement | Priority | Surfaces |

Partial-credit checks: (a) uses vocabulary present in `client_ai_memory` category='tone' or 'terminology'; (b) avoids the forbidden-word list from `client_ai_memory` category='avoid'; (c) register stays consistent from first to last paragraph (no mid-piece drift). **PASS ≥ 4, FAIL ≤ 2.** | **LLM Judge** (Claude Sonnet 4.6 scoring brief/chat against the client's `client_ai_memory` snapshot, with 3 labeled few-shot pairs per client archetype) + **Human calibration** (Senior Copywriter + Account Manager re-score 20% sample; target ≥ 0.7 correlation with judge per `ai-evals.md`). | **Critical** | Brief, Chat |

**Wiring OTEL into `app/Services/Ai/*`:**

```php
// app/Services/Ai/Tracing/AiTracer.php — single tracer, reused across all services.
use OpenTelemetry\API\Globals;
use OpenTelemetry\API\Trace\SpanKind;
use OpenTelemetry\SemConv\TraceAttributes;

final class AiTracer
{
    public function wrap(string $capability, array $attrs, callable $op): mixed
    {
        $tracer = Globals::tracerProvider()->getTracer('briefy-ai');
        $span = $tracer->spanBuilder("ai.{$capability}")
            ->setSpanKind(SpanKind::KIND_CLIENT)
            ->setAttribute('ai.capability', $capability)
            ->setAttribute('ai.model', $attrs['model'] ?? 'unknown')
            ->setAttribute('ai.prompt_version_hash', $attrs['prompt_hash'] ?? null)
            ->setAttribute('briefy.client_id', $attrs['client_id'] ?? null)
            ->setAttribute('briefy.demand_id', $attrs['demand_id'] ?? null)
            ->startSpan();
        try {
            $result = $op($span);
            // Attach usage after the call — works for both streamed and blocking paths.
            if (isset($attrs['usage'])) {
                $span->setAttribute('ai.input_tokens', $attrs['usage']->input_tokens);
                $span->setAttribute('ai.output_tokens', $attrs['usage']->output_tokens);
                $span->setAttribute('ai.cache_read_tokens', $attrs['usage']->cache_read_input_tokens ?? 0);
                $span->setAttribute('ai.cache_write_tokens', $attrs['usage']->cache_creation_input_tokens ?? 0);
            }
            return $result;
        } catch (\Throwable $e) {
            $span->recordException($e);
            $span->setStatus(\OpenTelemetry\API\Trace\StatusCode::STATUS_ERROR);
            throw $e;
        } finally {
            $span->end();
        }
    }
}
```

Every `AiBriefController`, `AiChatController`, `MonthlyPlanGenerator`, `ClientMemoryExtractor`, `ConversationCompactor`, and `ItemRedesigner` call wraps its `messages->create*` invocation with `AiTracer::wrap()`. Phoenix then shows the full picture: capability, model, prompt hash, tokens in/out, cache hit flag, latency, and (once eval results are written) the dimension scores side-by-side.

- **Interactive labeling tool:** `php artisan ai:eval:label` walks through unlabeled examples in the terminal: shows input, shows generated output, prompts for score per dimension + free-text notes, writes to `ai_eval_examples.human_scores`.
- **LLM-judge calibration gate:** before trusting an LLM-judge score in CI, it must reach **≥ 0.7 Spearman correlation** with the human labels on the reference dataset (per `ai-evals.md`). Recalibrate any time the judge prompt or judge model changes.
- **Flywheel:** every production interaction flagged by online guardrails or by sampling (Section 6 offline row) becomes a *candidate* for the dataset. Promotion rule: after a human labels it, it's added to `ai-eval-dataset.jsonl` with a `source: "production"` flag. Target: dataset grows by ≥ 5 examples/month during pilot.

## 6. Guardrails

> Online guardrails run inline in the PHP request path — they must be cheap (schema validation, regex, count check, budget counter). Expensive LLM-judge checks live in the offline flywheel. Guardrails minimize — each one adds latency and a failure mode of its own.

### Online (Real-Time)

Runs inline in `app/Services/Ai/*` before returning output to the user. If the intervention is "Block", the user sees a friendly retry message and the original attempt is logged to `ai_evaluations` with `verdict=fail, sampled_from_production=true`.

| # | Guardrail | Trigger (runs where) | Cost | Intervention |
|---|-----------|----------------------|------|--------------|
| G1 | **Monthly-plan schema gate** | On every `MonthlyPlanGenerator::generate()` response, before writing `planning_suggestions`. Runs the `MonthlyPlanSchema::rules()` Laravel validator + the deterministic `QuotaChecker` (exact count, date range, channel breakdown match). | ~1 ms | **Block + auto-retry up to 3×** with the validation error fed back to the model (per Section 4b retry pattern). After 3 failures, surface "Não foi possível gerar o planejamento. Tente novamente." to the user. |
| G2 | **Memory-write scope + PII check** | On every `ClientMemoryExtractor` completion, before upserting to `client_ai_memory`. Asserts: (a) every insight's target `client_id` equals the conversation's `context.client_id`; (b) `confidence ≥ 0.6`; (c) no PII regex match (CPF `\d{3}\.\d{3}\.\d{3}-\d{2}`, CNPJ, e-mail, phone). | ~5 ms (regex) | **Block the offending insight** (drop silently from upsert), keep the compliant rest. Log to `ai_evaluations` with `dimension=memory_scope_integrity, verdict=fail`. |
| G3 | **Per-request token ceiling** | Before dispatching any `messages->create*` call, the `AiTracer::wrap()` pre-check estimates input tokens (system + messages + expected output via `max_tokens`) using `\Anthropic\Core\TokenCounter` (SDK-provided utility). | ~1 ms | **Block** with HTTP 413 if estimated tokens exceed per-capability ceiling (brief: 15k in / 2k out; chat: 35k in / 2k out; plan: 12k in / 4k out). Log spike for investigation (usually a symptom of runaway `client_ai_memory` or un-compacted chat history). |
| G4 | **Streaming sanity gate** | Inside the SSE generator, on `message_stop` event, before persisting `ai_conversation_messages` or `demands.ai_analysis.brief`. Asserts: `len(assistant) > 10` chars AND `assistant` does not equal `"Error"` / API refusal sentinel. | <1 ms | **Flag + escalate**: persist the row but mark `ai_conversation_messages.quality_flag = 'short_or_empty'`. UI shows the message normally but the flag surfaces in the offline flywheel for human review. Does NOT block — a truncated stream is already a worse UX than showing what we got. |
| G5 | **Monthly cost budget per org** | On every AI call, `AiUsageMeter` middleware increments the organization's monthly token spend (in Redis, read-through from `ai_usage_monthly` table). Compare to hard cap (default $50/month/org, overridable per plan tier). | ~2 ms (Redis) | **Block with HTTP 429** + friendly message "Limite mensal de IA atingido. Entre em contato." when cap hit. Warning banner at 80% cap. This is the only budget-enforcement layer in Phase 3 — billing-plan quotas are deferred. |
| G6 | **Sector-compliance keyword sniff** (secondary guardrail per Section 1b regulatory context) | On every **brief** stream completion, regex-scan the output for high-risk phrases: "cientificamente comprovado", "garantia de retorno", "cura", "100% eficaz", "melhor do mercado", "aprovado pela ANVISA" (when client has no evidence in memory). | ~2 ms | **Flag** (not block) — add a `warnings` array to `demands.ai_analysis.brief_warnings` JSON field. UI renders a yellow banner "Esta frase pode precisar de revisão de compliance" above the flagged sentence. Account Manager decides. |

**Explicitly NOT online guardrails** (too expensive for the hot path; run offline in Section 6 flywheel): LLM-judge brand-voice scoring, LLM-judge groundedness auditing, full pillar-diversity scoring.

### Offline (Flywheel)

Runs as scheduled artisan jobs against production interactions. Feeds the `ai_evaluations` table, Phoenix dashboards, and the dataset-promotion process.

| Metric | Sampling Strategy | Cadence | Action on Degradation |
|--------|-------------------|---------|----------------------|
| **Brand voice fit (D1)** | **Weighted sampling** via `ai:eval:sample-prod`: every brief/chat turn from a client where the Account Manager flagged at least one previous output that week = 100%; all briefs with ≥ 1 regeneration in same session = 50% sample; remaining briefs = 10% sample. Judged by the calibrated LLM judge; 20% of flagged samples re-reviewed by Senior Copywriter. | Nightly job `php artisan ai:eval:sample-prod --dim=brand_voice_fit` | If rolling 7-day mean score drops below 4.0 → create a GitHub issue auto-assigned to founder with the 10 worst-scoring examples + their prompt hash. If drop coincides with a `prompt_version_hash` change → revert prompt. |
| **Context grounding (D6)** | All chat turns where response contains one of: explicit reference verbs ("lembra", "na conversa", "no comentário", "no arquivo", "mês passado", "anteriormente") — these are the high-risk turns for hallucination. Judged by the groundedness auditor (Haiku 4.5). | Nightly | If hallucination flag rate > 2% of flagged-verb turns over 7 days → pause `ExtractClientMemoryJob` auto-writes (memory poisoning risk) and escalate. |
| **Pillar diversity (D5)** | Every generated monthly plan (small volume — 100% sample). | Per-generation (within 5 min, async) | If rolling mean score drops below 4.0 for 2 consecutive plans → revisit `planSystem()` few-shot block and seasonal-calendar injection. |
| **Memory-write confidence distribution** | Every `ClientMemoryExtractor` output. Histogram of `confidence` scores per client, per week. | Weekly | If > 30% of accepted writes have `confidence ∈ [0.6, 0.7]` (borderline) → raise the drop-threshold to 0.7 for that client. If confidence mean drops week-over-week → inspect recent chat transcripts for off-topic conversations polluting extraction. |
| **Cache hit-rate (chat turns 2..N)** | All chat turns with `turn_index > 1` on active conversations. Aggregated from OTEL span attributes `ai.cache_read_tokens` vs. `ai.cache_write_tokens`. | Hourly rollup in Phoenix | If < 70% cache-hit rate over 24h → alert: likely cause is a byte-drift in the cached prefix (timestamp leak, memory-ordering change). Inspect `prompt_version_hash` changes in the last 24h. |
| **Regeneration rate per brief** | Every `ai_analysis.brief` write where `brief_generated_at` is the 2nd+ timestamp for the same demand (stored as a counter in `demands.ai_analysis.regeneration_count`). | Daily | If regeneration rate > 40% over 7 days → signal that users are dissatisfied with first-pass output. Feeds directly into the dataset (every brief regenerated ≥ 2× gets promoted to the reference dataset as a "hard case"). |
| **Cost per active client (rolling 30d)** | Sum of OTEL span `ai.input_tokens * price + ai.output_tokens * price` grouped by `briefy.client_id`. | Daily | If any client's monthly cost exceeds 2× the org's average per-client cost → investigate for memory-bloat (long `client_ai_memory`, un-compacted conversations, runaway `max_tokens`). |

**Dataset promotion rule for the flywheel:** any production interaction that (a) scores ≤ 2 Likert on any Critical dimension, or (b) fails any Pass/Fail Critical check, or (c) is manually flagged by an Account Manager in-app, becomes a labeling candidate. `php artisan ai:eval:label --source=production` surfaces the queue. Promoted examples are tagged `source: "production"` in the dataset JSONL.

## 7. Production Monitoring

**Tracing Tool:** **Arize Phoenix** (self-hosted Docker container in the same VPC as the Laravel app; OTLP gRPC ingestion on :4317). Every AI call from `app/Services/Ai/*` emits one OTEL span via `AiTracer::wrap()`. Spans carry: `ai.capability`, `ai.model`, `ai.prompt_version_hash`, `ai.input_tokens`, `ai.output_tokens`, `ai.cache_read_tokens`, `ai.cache_write_tokens`, `ai.latency_ms`, `briefy.organization_id`, `briefy.client_id`, `briefy.demand_id`, `briefy.user_id`, `ai.regeneration_count` (brief/chat only), `ai.conversation_compacted` (chat only). Eval results from `php artisan ai:eval:sample-prod` write back as span events on the same trace ID so Phoenix shows traces + scores in one view.

Fallback / additional sinks:
- **Laravel logs** (`storage/logs/ai.log` via a dedicated channel) for every SDK error, retry, and schema-validation failure. Retained 30 days.
- **`ai_evaluations` table** for every eval run (CI + nightly + production sampling). Joined to `ai_usage_monthly` for cost dashboards.

**Key Metrics to Track:**

1. **Latency p95 per capability** (seconds) — time-to-first-token for streaming (brief, chat); total duration for non-streaming (plan, redesign, memory extraction). Sourced from span duration minus the generator setup overhead.
2. **Cache hit rate on chat turns 2..N** (%) — `sum(cache_read_tokens) / sum(cache_read_tokens + cache_write_tokens + uncached_input_tokens)`. Indirect signal for per-turn cost.
3. **Error rate per capability** (% of calls returning HTTP 5xx, timing out, or raising `APIStatusException`). Broken into: `RateLimitException`, `APITimeoutException`, `APIConnectionException`, schema-validation failures, streaming truncation.
4. **Monthly token spend per organization** (USD equivalent, rolling 30d) — feeds budget guardrail G5 and dashboards for pricing / plan-tier decisions.
5. **Quality scores per dimension** (from flywheel) — rolling 7-day mean for each of Dimensions 1, 5, 6, 8. Plotted per-client to surface specific brand-voice drift. Pass-rate for Dimensions 2, 3, 4, 7, 9, 10, 11.
6. **Regeneration rate per brief** (%) — primary user-dissatisfaction proxy. Distinct from error rate: a brief that streams successfully but the user discards and regenerates is a silent quality failure the infrastructure metrics miss.
7. **Memory-write confidence distribution** (histogram) — watches for drift toward low-confidence pollution. Separate series per extraction category (tone, patterns, preferences, avoid, terminology).

**Alert Thresholds** (wired to Slack #alerts-ai via Phoenix alerting + Laravel Horizon exception notifier):

| Severity | Condition | Action |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| **Page (P1)** | Error rate for brief or chat > 5% over 15 min | Page on-call (founder) |
| **Page (P1)** | Memory-scope-integrity guardrail (G2) blocks > 0.5% of extractions over 1 hour | Page — potential bug in `ExtractClientMemoryJob` scoping |
| **Page (P1)** | Monthly cost budget (G5) blocks ≥ 3 distinct orgs in 24h | Page — likely a plan-tier mismatch or runaway usage pattern |
| **Alert (P2)** | Latency p95 (brief) > 12s OR latency p95 (chat TTFT) > 2.5s for 30 min | Slack alert — investigate model, cache, network |
| **Alert (P2)** | Cache hit rate on chat turns 2..N < 70% over 1 hour | Slack alert — likely a cached-prefix byte drift; check recent `prompt_version_hash` |
| **Alert (P2)** | Quality score for brand_voice_fit (D1) rolling 7d mean < 4.0 | Slack alert with link to 10 worst examples in Phoenix |
| **Alert (P2)** | Quota compliance (D4) pass-rate < 100% over 1 day | Slack alert — quota violations should be zero; any miss is investigated |
| **Alert (P2)** | Streaming reliability (D9) pass-rate < 99% over 1 day | Slack alert — inspect buffering config, SDK version, SSE proxy path |
| **Warn (P3)** | Regeneration rate per brief > 40% over 7 days | Create GitHub issue, no page |
| **Warn (P3)** | Memory-write confidence mean drifts > 0.1 downward week-over-week | Create GitHub issue; weekly review |

**Smart Sampling Strategy** (for human review — concentrate eyeballs on signal):

Phoenix "sample for review" query, run nightly and surfaced via `php artisan ai:eval:sample-prod --for-review`. Union of:

- **100% of regenerations** — if a user regenerated a brief, the first attempt was unsatisfactory. Auto-promote both to the labeling queue.
- **100% of conversations with > 15 messages** — long conversations show compaction artifacts, voice drift, and cache-miss patterns that short ones hide.
- **100% of chat turns with `cache_read_tokens = 0` and `turn_index > 1`** — cache misses are either a bug (prefix drift) or a signal of session churn worth reviewing.
- **100% of plan generations where schema retry occurred** — retries mean the model fought the quota/channel constraints; these are the hardest prompts.
- **All hallucination-verb flagged chat turns** (from flywheel row 2) — highest signal for Dimension 6 degradation.
- **20% random sample of "clean" interactions** — baseline to detect slow-moving quality drift that doesn't trip any filter (the most dangerous failure mode — silent and cumulative).
- **All Account-Manager-flagged interactions** — a flag button in the DemandDetailModal lets the AM tag an AI output as "off" with a reason (drift / hallucination / other). These are gold-standard negative labels and go straight to the top of the review queue.

Daily review batch target: ≤ 25 interactions to keep the Account Manager / Senior Copywriter review budget bounded (≈ 30 min/day). Samples over budget are re-prioritized by criticality.
